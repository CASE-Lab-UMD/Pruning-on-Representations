Demystifying When Pruning Works via Representation Hierarchies

A representation-level view of why pruning can preserve non-generative behavior while still harming autoregressive generation.

Shuai He, Guoheng Sun, Haichao Zhang, Yun Fu, Ang Li
University of Maryland, College Park and Northeastern University

Paper Code BibTeX

Core question

When does pruning preserve capability, and when does it break generation?

We study pruning through a hierarchy of representations: hidden states, logits, and probability distributions. The same pruned model can appear stable in one space while showing larger deviations in another.

Representation hierarchy Pruning effects are compared across hidden, logit, and probability spaces instead of relying on a single metric.
Evaluation mismatch Fixed-target evaluations can remain stable even when autoregressive decoding becomes fragile.
Generation dynamics Small distributional shifts can accumulate across decoding steps and change final generations.

Evidence

Pruning looks different across spaces and decoding regimes.

The analysis connects non-generative robustness, representation-space sensitivity, and generation-time divergence under inter-layer and intra-layer pruning.
